### ğŸš€ Launch Day: Reddit (Build in Public / Maker Story)
**Subreddits**: r/SideProject, r/IndieHackers, r/Biohackers

**Title:**
**I was getting anxiety from my "Wellness" apps, so I built an AI Agent to intercept the data. (Next.js + LLM)**

**Body:**
Hi Reddit,
I'm a dev who has been obsessed with Quantified Self for years.
But recently I hit a wall: **Dashboard Fatigue.**
Opening my health app felt like checking a bank account when you're broke. The raw data ("Sleep: 4h", "HRV: 20ms") was just creating a cortisol feedback loop.

**So I built a "Headless" Health App.**
It's called **AntiAnxiety**.

**The Stack:**
- **Frontend**: Next.js (Visuals are procedural Canvas flow, no charts).
- **Backend**: Supabase.
- **AI**: LLM Agent ("Max") that ingests health data via API.

**How it works:**
Instead of showing me a dashboard, "Max" runs in the background.
It analyzes the trend. If I'm good, it says nothing.
If my physiological stress spikes, it intercepts.
It sends a push notification with a specific, actionable micro-intervention (e.g., "Physiological Sigh" breathing guide) tailored to my real-time biometrics.

**It connects to Oura & Apple Health.**
I'm launching the MVP today. It's built for people who want the insights without the judgment.

Would love your feedback on the "No Access to Data" philosophy. Is it too radical?
